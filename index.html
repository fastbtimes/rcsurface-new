Pros & Cons of Dry Ice Blasting

Understanding the advantages and limitations of different cleaning methods.

Advantages of Dry Ice Blasting

  • Eco-Friendly

    No chemicals, water, or secondary waste. CO2 sublimates into gas.

  • Safe for Equipment

    Non-abrasive - won't damage surfaces or delicate components.

  • Non-Conductive

    Safe to use on electrical equipment and live circuits.

  • Non-Flammable

    CO2 is inert - safe for flammable environments.

  • No Cleanup

    No secondary waste - just sweep up the removed debris.

  • Bacterial Control

    Cold temperature kills bacteria and mold on contact.

  • Carbon Neutral

    Uses recycled CO2 - no new carbon emissions.

Limitations to Consider

  • Cold Temperature

    -109°F dry ice can freeze materials. Not suitable for all applications.

  • Ventilation Required

    CO2 gas buildup requires proper ventilation in enclosed spaces.

  • Noise Level

    High-pressure blasting can be loud - hearing protection required.

  • Higher Cost

    Dry ice pellets and specialized equipment make it more expensive than traditional methods.

  • Supply Chain

    Requires steady supply of dry ice pellets for continuous operation.

  • Not for All Materials

    May damage soft materials like certain plastics or rubber at close range.
